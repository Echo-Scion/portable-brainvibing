# Resource: Startup Growth & Sprint Planning (`startup_growth.md`)

This resource codifies the strategic business logic and operational rhythm for projects initialized with the `--startup` flag. It bridges the gap between technical architecture and venture-ready execution.

---

## 🚀 1. The Investor's Lens (Pitch Logic)
When synthesizing Chapter 1-3 of the Master Blueprint, enforce these "Pitch-Ready" interrogation points:
- **The "Why Now?"**: Why is this solution critical at this exact moment in the market?
- **Unfair Advantage**: Identify the "Moat" (Data, Network Effect, or Technical IP).
- **Revenue Engine**: Define the path to $1M ARR (Subscriptions, Transactional, or Enterprise).
- **Exit Strategy**: Potential acquisition targets or long-term ecosystem positioning.

---

## 📅 2. The Operational Pulse (Sprint Planning)
When synthesizing Chapter 6 (Execution Roadmap), translate MVP phases into actionable Sprints:
- **Sprint 0 (The Foundation)**: Core infra, schema, and Auth.
- **Sprint 1 (The "Magic Moment")**: Implementation of the single core feature that proves value.
- **Sprint 2 (The Feedback Loop)**: Analytics, basic CRM, and user onboarding.
- **Sprint 3 (The Polish)**: High-end UI (`ui-finish`) and performance audit.

---

## 🛠️ 3. Startup Metrics (KPI Framework)
For every startup feature, define its "North Star" metric:
- **Acquisition**: CPA (Cost Per Acquisition).
- **Activation**: Time to "Aha!" moment.
- **Retention**: LTV (Lifetime Value) vs. Churn.
- **Referral**: Virality Coefficient (K-factor).

---

## ⚖️ 4. Execution Guardrails
- **Build vs. Buy**: Favor integrated services (Supabase, Stripe, Firebase) for non-core features to maximize speed.
- **The 80/20 Rule**: Focus 80% of effort on the 20% of features that drive 80% of the value.
- **Scalability vs. Premature Optimization**: Design for 10k users, but don't over-engineer for 10M until Sprint 4.
